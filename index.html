Splatting in the Wild

Congrong Xu,
ShanghaiTech University, UC Berkeley
Paper (in progress) Code (in progress)

Abstract

Novel view synthesis from unconstrained in-the-wild images remains a significant yet challenging task due to photometric variations and transient occluders that complicate accurate scene reconstruction. Traditional methods have attempted to address these issues by integrating global appearance features in Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF). Although 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) offers faster training and real-time rendering, it struggles with in-the-wild scenarios. Recent advancements, such as SWAG and GS-W, have improved 3DGS but still exhibit limitations. In this paper, we introduce Splatfacto-W, a novel approach that leverages the strengths of these methods while incorporating a neural background model to enhance multiview consistency and effectively represent backgrounds. Our key contributions include latent appearance modeling, efficient transient object handling, and precise background modeling. Extensive experiments demonstrate that Splatfacto-W outperforms existing methods in in-the-wild scenarios, delivering high-quality, real-time novel view synthesis with improved scene consistency.

Appearance Embedding Interpolation

The latent representation of a scene is in a continuous space, which allows interpolating between two embeddings smoothly captures variation in appearance without affecting 3D geometry.

Real-time Changing Appearance in Viewer

You can change the appearance of the scene to any image real-time in the viewer.



Neural Background Model

The background model is a three-layer mlp that predicts the background of the scene without introducing any 2d model. The background model can be trained effectively along with the gaussian splatting and cleans up the floters in the scene.

A new alpha loss term is introduced to prevent the gaussians from occupying the background.
